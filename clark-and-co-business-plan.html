Revenue Streams

Three channels. Each one compounds differently. Build all three.

Stream 01
Direct to Consumer
Website, Instagram, farmers markets. Highest margin per bag. Slowest to scale.
Margin per bag
~$9–11
Stream 02
Subscriptions
Monthly recurring. Predictable income. The backbone of a sustainable roastery.
Margin per subscriber/mo
~$14–18
Stream 03
Wholesale
Local cafés, restaurants, offices. Lower margin but volume. Transforms the numbers fast.
Margin per kg wholesale
~$12–16

The magic formula: subscriptions give you a floor (you know next month's income), wholesale gives you volume (3 café accounts = 60kg/month extra), and direct-to-consumer gives you margin. Build all three simultaneously from day one.

Launch Checklist

Everything you need to do before your first sale. Check things off as you go.

0 of 24 complete
Legal & Operations
Do first
  • Register LLC — Clark & Co. Coffee Roasters LLC. Illinois filing is around $150. Use LegalZoom or file direct with the IL Secretary of State.
    Legal
  • Open business bank account — keep finances separate from day one. Makes taxes far easier.
    Legal
  • Get a food handler's license — required in Illinois to sell food products. Apply through the Chicago Dept of Public Health.
    Legal
  • Check home roasting regulations — confirm your home setup meets Chicago's cottage food laws. Consult CDPH if unsure.
    Legal
  • Get business insurance — product liability coverage. Around $500/year for small food businesses.
    Legal
Equipment & Sourcing
Get roasting
  • Buy Fresh Roast SR800 — from Sweet Maria's for the free green bean bundle. ~$289.
    Ops
  • Order first green bean samples — Sweet Maria's has a sample pack program. Try 6–8 origins before committing to stock.
    Ops
  • Set up roasting space — dedicated circuit, ventilation, fire extinguisher, smoke detector. Garage is ideal.
    Ops
  • Buy packaging — resealable kraft bags with one-way valve, labels. Pacific Bag Inc or CustomCupsFob for small quantities.
    Ops
  • Get a decent kitchen scale — 0.1g precision for weighing green and roasted batches consistently.
    Ops
  • Set up roast logging — even a simple spreadsheet tracking date, origin, batch weight, temp profile, and cupping notes.
    Ops

Brand
